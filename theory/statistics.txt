DAY 1 

🔹Statistics = The branch of mathematics that deals with collecting,
  organizing, analyzing, and interpreting data to make decisions or understand patterns.

🔹 Descriptive Statistics

i.Descriptive

👉 It describes and summarize data.
Summarizes and organizes information.
Examples: mean, median, mode, charts, percentages.
📌 Example:
“In a class of 50 students, the average mark is 70.”

ii. Inferential Statistics

👉 It predicts or makes conclusions from data.
Uses a sample to guess about a larger population.
Examples: hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, regression.
📌 Example:
“From a survey of 100 voters, we predict the winner of the election.”

DAY 2 

🔹 Statistical Data

Data = information collected for analysis.
Types:
Qualitative (Categorical data) → words/labels
Example: Gender (Male/Female), Blood group (A, B, O).
Quantitative (Numerical data) → numbers
Example: Height, Weight, Marks.
Can be:
Discrete (countable, e.g., number of students)
Continuous (measurable, e.g., height, temperature)

🔹 Statistical Measurements

These are ways to summarize and understand data.
Measures of Central Tendency → show the "center" of data
Mean (average)
Median (middle value)
Mode (most frequent value)
Measures of Dispersion (Spread) → show how data is spread out
Range (max – min)
Variance (average of squared differences from mean)
Standard Deviation (SD) (spread of data around mean)
Measures of Position → show relative standing
Percentiles, Quartiles, Deciles

🔹Scales of measurement

1. Nominal Scale (Name only)
Labels or categories (no order, no numbers).
Example: Gender (Male/Female), Blood group (A, B, O).
2. Ordinal Scale (Order matters)
Categories with a ranking/order, but differences are not exact.
Example: Position in a race (1st, 2nd, 3rd), Ratings (Good, Better, Best).
3. Interval Scale (Order + equal gaps, no true zero)
Numerical scale with equal intervals, but zero does not mean “nothing.”
Example: Temperature in Celsius or Fahrenheit (0°C doesn’t mean no temperature).
4. Ratio Scale (Order + equal gaps + true zero)
Like interval scale, but has a true zero (zero = nothing).
Example: Height, Weight, Age, Income.

DAY 3 

🔹Population vs Sampling 

1. population - every data points in a group

2. sample - is a subset of population

🔹Sampling Techniques

1.Random Sampling 🎲
Every member has an equal chance of being chosen.
Example: Picking names from a hat.

2.Systematic Sampling 🔢
Select every kth member from a list.
Example: Choosing every 10th student in a roll list.

3.Stratified Sampling 📊
Divide the population into groups (strata) based on characteristics, then take samples from each group.
Example: Sampling students by grade level.

4.Cluster Sampling 🏘️
Divide population into clusters (groups), then randomly select some clusters and study everyone in them.
Example: Choosing a few schools and surveying all students there.

5.Convenience Sampling 🛒
Choosing the sample that is easy to reach.

Example: Asking your friends for a survey.

ii.alternative hypothesis - other than null hypothesis (H1)

     alternative hypothesis 'the population mean is not 65'
